# Departments & Organizational Structure

Faysal Bank, like most full-service commercial banks, is organized into customer-facing business lines and internal support/control functions. Below is a breakdown relevant to the 2010–2020 period, based on publicly available organizational information.

## 1. Business / Revenue-Generating Departments

### Retail Banking
Handles individual customer accounts, savings and current accounts, consumer lending, and branch-level services. This was the department most directly affected by the RBS acquisition (2010) and the subsequent Islamic conversion, since branch-level products had to be redesigned as Shariah-compliant offerings.

### Corporate Banking
Serves large corporate clients with lending, trade finance, cash management, and advisory services. This division manages relationships with big businesses and institutional clients.

### Commercial & SME Banking
Focuses on small and medium enterprises, offering working capital finance, trade facilities, and business accounts — a key growth segment for Pakistani banks during this decade as SBP pushed for greater SME financial inclusion.

### Consumer Banking
Covers personal loans, credit/debit cards, auto finance, and home finance products marketed directly to individual consumers.

### Islamic Banking Division
Grew from a smaller unit into the bank's central strategic focus following the 2014 announcement. Responsible for developing Shariah-compliant versions of all conventional products (current/savings accounts, financing, cards, etc.) under Islamic finance principles.

### Treasury & Trade / Business Services
Manages the bank's liquidity, foreign exchange operations, investment portfolio, and trade-related banking services (letters of credit, guarantees, etc.).

### Wealth Management / Priority Banking
Provides investment advisory, priority banking services, and wealth products for high-net-worth clients.

## 2. Support & Control Functions (Head Office)

Consistent with standard bank head-office structures, Faysal Bank's central functions during this period included:

- **Strategic Planning** — long-term direction-setting, including the Islamic conversion roadmap
- **Finance & Accounts** — financial reporting, budgeting, regulatory financial disclosures
- **Human Resources (HR)** — workforce management, notably active during the post-2010 restructuring/downsizing phase
- **Information Technology (IT)** — core banking systems, and later, digital banking infrastructure
- **Legal & Compliance** — regulatory compliance, particularly significant given the 2020 SBP fine
- **Risk Management** — credit risk, operational risk, and market risk oversight
- **Corporate Communications & Marketing** — brand management, including the "Faysal Islami" brand rollout
- **Procurement (CPO)** — vendor and supply chain management for bank operations
- **Tax** — tax planning and regulatory tax compliance

## 3. Governance & Oversight

### Shariah Board
As the bank transitioned to Islamic banking, its Shariah Board became increasingly central to governance — reviewing and approving product structures, processes, and operations for Shariah compliance. Board members during and after this period have included scholars with backgrounds in Islamic finance, fiqh, and financial law.

### Board of Directors
Provides overall governance and strategic oversight, including approval of major decisions such as the RBS acquisition and the Islamic conversion strategy.
