# Executive Summary

**Project:** A researched profile of Faysal Bank Limited's strategic transformation from 2010 to 2020, covering organizational structure, financial performance, and a critical evaluation of its decade-long pivot to Islamic banking.

## The One-Paragraph Version

In 2010, Faysal Bank acquired the Pakistan operations of RBS, inheriting scale but also integration costs and an oversized workforce. Rather than simply absorbing this, bank leadership used the following decade to pursue a deliberate strategic pivot: converting the entire bank to Shariah-compliant Islamic banking, a process announced in 2014 and substantially executed by 2020 (fully completed by 2023). Audited financial data confirms the strategy coincided with a real acceleration in performance — profit after tax grew at a 20.9% CAGR from 2011-2018, roughly double the 9.7% deposit growth rate — while the branch network nearly doubled from 257 to 576 locations. The transformation was not without cost: a Rs. 96.1 million SBP regulatory fine in 2020 suggests compliance functions were under strain during the most intensive period of change.

## Key Findings

| Question | Finding |
|---|---|
| Did the bank grow? | Yes — total assets grew from PKR 293bn (2011) to PKR 710bn (2020), a >140% increase |
| Did profitability improve? | Yes, substantially — profit after tax grew ~5x from 2011 to 2020, outpacing asset growth |
| Was the Islamic conversion timed well? | Evidence suggests yes — profit growth accelerated specifically from 2014, the year of the conversion announcement, after a flat 2011-2013 |
| How did it compare to Meezan Bank (the market leader)? | Meezan grew deposits/assets ~2.5x faster (24.5% vs 9.7% CAGR), but Faysal's profit grew faster (20.9% vs 14.9% CAGR) — Faysal converted growth into profit more efficiently, even while remaining smaller |
| Was execution flawless? | No — the conversion took longer than the originally stated 3-5 year timeline (completed 2023, not ~2017-2019), and a compliance fine occurred in 2020 |
| Is this a definitive causal story? | No — the data shows strong correlation between strategy and performance, not proof of causation. See [08-critical-analysis](../08-critical-analysis/analysis.md) for the full reasoning. |
